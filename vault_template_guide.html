Industry Knowledge Vault

Each vault is a structured knowledge base that teaches the AI pipeline how to think about an industry — the analytical frameworks, competitive dynamics, and leading indicators that a sector specialist carries in their head. A living layer keeps the vault current with earnings intelligence, material events, and weekly industry briefs. Structural knowledge feeds the AI; current-state intelligence serves the PM. Both update automatically with date-range scoping.

📚

Vault = How to Think  |  Pipeline = What Is Happening

A vault contains structural industry knowledge that changes slowly (quarterly at most): value chain layers, profit pool maps, demand segmentation frameworks, competitive relationships, leading indicators.

The live pipeline provides current-state data: latest quarter financials, stock prices, consensus estimates, recent news. At analysis time, the pipeline combines the vault's framework with fresh data — so the AI knows both how to decompose an industry AND what the current numbers are.

2  /  The 7 Structural Files

Each file answers one specific question an analyst would ask when learning a new sector. All examples from the Data Center vault.

Feeds Pipeline
_overview.md
"What is this industry and how is it structured?"
Industry thesis, sub-segment definitions, total addressable market, and binding constraints.
  • Defines the sub-segments the pipeline uses to classify companies (GPU cloud, colocation, optical, chips, cooling)
  • States the industry's binding constraint — for DC: power availability, not demand
  • Sizes the market with sourced estimates (e.g., $350B DC TAM by 2028)
DC vault example: "The primary constraint on data center expansion is power availability, not demand. Grid interconnection queues in key markets (PJM, ERCOT) run 4-7 years, creating a structural bottleneck that favors operators with secured power capacity."
Feeds Pipeline
_value_chain.md
"Where does the money flow and who captures value?"
Layer-by-layer profit pool map with margin ranges, competitive dynamics, and cross-layer dependencies.
  • Maps every layer from silicon to hyperscale with operating margin ranges
  • Identifies which layers have pricing power vs commodity dynamics
  • Shows cross-layer dependencies (e.g., "neocloud margins compress when GPU supply normalizes")
DC vault layers: L1 Compute Silicon (20-30% margins) → L2 Networking & Interconnect (5-25%) → L3 Colocation (10-20%) → L4 Neoclouds (5-20%) → L5 Hyperscale Platforms (25-35%). The margin range itself is analytical signal — 5-25% at L2 means profitability depends entirely on where in the value chain the company sits.
Feeds Pipeline
_demand_composition.md
"What drives demand and how should the AI decompose revenue?"
Structural demand segmentation with shift mechanisms and a revenue bridge decomposition guide that the AI uses when building company-specific models.
  • Lists segmentation axes (e.g., by customer type: hyperscale, enterprise, sovereign)
  • Explains composition shift mechanisms — HOW demand mix changes affect specific companies
  • Provides a revenue bridge guide — tells the AI what sub-segments to break revenue into
  • Contains NO current percentages or growth rates (those come from live pipeline data)
DC vault example: "AI training demand is concentrated in GPU-dense compute (L4), while AI inference demand is distributed across colocation (L3) and edge. A shift from training-heavy to inference-heavy workloads compresses neocloud utilization but expands colocation opportunity." The AI uses this to decompose NBIS revenue by workload type rather than just reporting a single growth number.
Feeds Pipeline
_debates.md
"What do smart people disagree about in this industry?"
2-3 key industry debates with both sides presented at equal evidence quality, market lean, resolution timeline, and affected companies.
  • Both sides MUST have comparable evidence quality — one side with data + other side hand-waving = biased vault
  • States where consensus sits ("market lean") so the AI knows what's priced in
  • Identifies which companies benefit or suffer under each outcome
DC vault debate — "Hyperscaler CapEx Sustainability":
Bull: Enterprise AI adoption is <2% penetrated; $200B+ annual hyperscaler CapEx is early-innings investment, not a bubble
Bear: ROI scrutiny will slow spending; hyperscalers have historically cut CapEx 20-40% within 12 months when returns disappoint
Market lean: Bull (CapEx guidance still accelerating in Q4 2025 earnings)
Resolution: Next 2-3 earnings cycles will show ROI metrics
Affected: NBIS, CRWV, IREN (all negatively exposed to CapEx slowdown)
Partial — Synthesis Only
_signals.md
"What leading indicators should we watch, and what do combinations mean?"
5 leading indicators per vault with current direction (ACCELERATING / DECELERATING / STABLE), evidence, and a signal interaction map showing what combinations indicate.
  • Directions are hidden from perspective agents (prevents anchoring on vault's assessment)
  • Directions shown to synthesis agent only (informs final recommendation weighting)
  • Interaction map shows what signal combinations mean — more valuable than any single signal
DC vault signals: Hyperscaler CapEx Trajectory (ACCELERATING), GPU Supply-Demand Balance (DECELERATING toward equilibrium), Power Availability (DECELERATING — scarcity worsening), Enterprise AI Adoption (ACCELERATING), Interconnect Bandwidth Demand (ACCELERATING)

Interaction example: "CapEx ACCELERATING + Power DECELERATING = site scarcity premium rises, favoring operators with secured power (IREN, EQIX) over those still in buildout (NBIS expansion sites)."
Feeds Pipeline
_kpis.md
"How do you measure companies in each sub-segment?"
Sub-segment-specific metrics with benchmark ranges. Different business models need different KPIs — a one-size-fits-all metric set would miss what matters.
  • GPU Cloud (NBIS, IREN): contracted capacity (MW), utilization rate, $/GPU-hr, net revenue retention
  • Colocation (EQIX): power density (kW/rack), interconnection revenue %, lease duration, churn
  • Optical (LITE, COHR): design wins, wavelength count, port speed migration cycle
  • Includes benchmark ranges so the AI can assess whether a company's metrics are strong or weak
GPU Cloud KPI example: "Contracted capacity utilization >85% = healthy demand. Below 70% = potential over-build risk. Net revenue retention >120% = upsell dynamics working (customers buying more compute over time)."
Reference
_changelog.md
"What changed since the last pipeline run?"
Timestamped log of material updates. Enables the pipeline to detect what's new and focus the AI's attention on changed information rather than re-analyzing everything.
  • Entries include date, what changed, and why it matters
  • Supports four update modes: structural refresh, current-state brief, earnings intelligence, material events
Example entry: "2026-02-25: Added NBIS as full node (Layer 4, neocloud). $22-26B contracted backlog, Microsoft/Meta anchor contracts. Reason: pure-play GPU cloud, archetypal DC vault company."
PM Only — Does Not Feed Pipeline
_brief.md
"What's happening in this industry right now?"
Weekly current-state snapshot for the PM's sector allocation decisions. Material developments, ticker triggers, and company event flags. Deliberately excluded from the pipeline to prevent current-state anchoring.
  • Refreshed weekly via --brief-only with date-range scoping (only queries developments since last update)
  • Includes "Company Event Flags" — one-line summary of which nodes have recent developments since last brief
  • Identifies "Migration Candidates" — brief findings that should promote to structural files
  • Point-in-time snapshot, replaced each refresh — not cumulative
DC vault example: "Material Development #1: NVIDIA announces $2B strategic investments in Nebius and CoreWeave, signaling preferred neocloud ecosystem partners... Company Event Flags: NBIS (NVIDIA $2B investment, Mar 12), RIOT (AMD 75MW option exercise, Mar 12)."
3  /  Company Nodes — Full vs Light

Every company in the vault gets a node. The depth depends on whether this industry is the company's primary home.

Full Node — "This IS their industry"

✓ Value chain position (layer, sub-segment)
✓ Revenue model (how they make money)
✓ Relationship map (suppliers, customers, competitors)
✓ Competitive advantages (with verification tags)
✓ Competitive risks (with verification tags)
✓ Valuation methodology (which metrics apply)
✓ Key KPIs (company-specific watchlist)
✓ Business model narrative (full analytical write-up)
✓ Competitive position narrative (vs peers, moats, vulnerabilities)
✓ Key relationships (each with why it matters to thesis)
✓ Industry-specific notes (nuances unique to this company)
★ Recent developments (optional — material events between earnings, timestamped)
★ Earnings intelligence (optional — management guidance, Q&A with [DEFLECTION] flags, sequential changes)

Light Node — "They're visiting this industry"

~ Value chain position (if mappable to a layer)
× Revenue model
× Relationship map
× Competitive advantages / risks
× Valuation methodology
× Key KPIs
× Full analytical write-ups
× Financial metrics (computed from XBRL at runtime)
★ Role in industry (why they appear in this vault)
★ Key signal (what to monitor from this company)
★ Relevance mechanism (causal chain connecting to this industry)
★ Primary industry pointer (where full node lives)
★ Recent developments (optional — filtered to host vault relevance only)
★ Earnings intelligence (optional — filtered subset relevant to host industry)

5  /  Light Node Example — ENPH (Enphase Energy)

A residential solar company whose VPP platform creates a cross-industry connection to data center power constraints. The light node captures ONLY why ENPH matters to this vault — not its full business profile.

Light Node
ENPH
Enphase Energy, Inc. — Distributed Energy Resources
Structured Metadata
node_type: light
primary_industry: residential_solar
layer: [] (no direct DC layer mapping)
sub_segment: distributed_energy_resources
Role in Industry
"ENPH is the leading US residential microinverter and battery storage manufacturer with a growing VPP (virtual power plant) platform. Not a data center company — it's a light node because its distributed energy fleet represents a potential grid capacity relief mechanism for power-constrained data center markets."
Key Signal (What to Monitor)
  • VPP enrollment growth and grid services revenue — how fast can residential batteries be aggregated into firm, dispatchable capacity?
  • If VPP scales and utilities accept distributed batteries as reliable grid resources → alternative pathway to alleviating DC power constraints
  • Watch: VPP dispatch volumes, utility partnerships, hyperscaler-funded residential DER programs
Relevance Mechanism (The Cross-Industry Causal Chain)
  • The constraint: PJM grid interconnection queues run 4-7 years. PJM projected to add 31 GW of DC load vs only 28 GW new generation
  • The alternative: Distributed DER aggregation via VPP provides faster grid capacity relief than new transmission (months vs years)
  • The evidence: Sunrun's CalReady VPP already delivers 250 MW average (375 MW peak) from ~75,000 residential batteries in California
  • The thesis: ENPH's "Jensen Cake" pitch at CES 2026 — hyperscalers could subsidize residential solar+battery to free grid capacity for DC. No hyperscaler has done this yet, but the math works: new DC power costs $50K+ per 5 kW vs ~$10K for a subsidized residential battery
  • The scale: ~9 GWh US residential battery fleet today = ~3.3 GW dispatchable. Adding batteries to 2.5M existing PV-only homes could yield tens of GW
What's NOT Here (By Design)
  • No financial metrics — ENPH's revenue, margins, EPS are computed from XBRL data at pipeline runtime
  • No competitive analysis — that lives in ENPH's full node in the residential_solar vault
  • No supplier/customer/competitor maps — only the cross-industry relationship matters here
  • No stock recommendation — the vault provides analytical context, not conclusions

7  /  Update Modes — How the Vault Stays Current

Four CLI modes keep the vault current at different cadences. Each mode writes to different files, feeds different consumers, and auto-commits (or doesn't) based on review requirements. All modes use date-range scoping — queries only fetch information since the last update, reducing cost and avoiding re-discovery.

Structural Refresh
--update
Quarterly. Reads all 7 structural files, diffs against current Perplexity research. Writes ONLY material changes — "factually wrong or structurally incomplete." A mature vault should return mostly no-changes. Stages changes but does NOT auto-commit — structural changes need human review before commit.
Feeds Pipeline Manual Commit
Industry Brief
--brief-only
Weekly. Writes current-state snapshot to _brief.md for PM sector allocation decisions. Includes material developments, ticker triggers, Company Event Flags (which nodes have recent events), and migration candidates for manual promotion to structural files. Does NOT feed the pipeline — prevents current-state anchoring.
PM Only Auto-Commit
Earnings Intelligence
--earnings NBIS
After each earnings report. Extracts call substance into the company node: management guidance, key Q&A exchanges with [DEFLECTION] flagging, competitive commentary, and sequential changes from prior quarter (STARTED/STOPPED/SHIFTED emphasis patterns). Two deep-research queries for full nodes, one for light nodes. Consolidates Recent Developments that earnings now supersede.
Feeds Pipeline Auto-Commit
Material Event
--event NBIS "NVIDIA $2B investment"
Ad-hoc, when material events occur between earnings. Appends a timestamped entry to the node's "Recent Developments" section. One targeted sonar-pro query (fast, cheap). Propagates filtered entries to light nodes in other vaults if relevant. Decision filter: run for events that change relationships, competitive position, or business model — NOT for price moves, analyst calls, or quarterly results.
Feeds Pipeline Auto-Commit
8  /  Key Design Decisions

Each decision addresses a specific failure mode we observed in practice.

No Current-State Data in Vault

Problem: Vault snapshots of prices, growth rates, or quarterly numbers go stale within weeks — and stale data silently biases analysis.
  • Vault contains only structural knowledge (changes quarterly at most)
  • Current data (prices, estimates, news) flows through the live pipeline at runtime
  • AI combines vault framework + fresh data at analysis time — never working from stale snapshots
  • Financial metrics for light nodes are computed from XBRL at runtime, not stored in vault

Signal Directions Hidden from Perspectives

Problem: If the bull analyst sees "CapEx = ACCELERATING," it anchors on the vault's judgment instead of forming its own from the evidence.
  • Perspective agents see the signal definitions (what to look for) but NOT the current direction
  • Forces perspectives to independently assess signal status from the live data in their fact set
  • Synthesis agent sees directions — uses them to weigh perspective conclusions, not to form the thesis
  • Prevents the vault from pre-determining the recommendation through upstream anchoring

[UNVERIFIED] Tags on Claims

Problem: AI-sourced industry data (via Perplexity) may contain errors that look authoritative. We need to distinguish verified from unverified.
  • Quantitative claims (capacity figures, revenue) stay [UNVERIFIED] — pipeline computes actuals from XBRL/API data at runtime
  • Structural claims (tier assignments, competitive relationships) need human review — these can't be self-corrected by the pipeline
  • Human review is ~30 min per vault, focused on structural accuracy, not forensic number-checking

Full Node vs Light Node Separation

Problem: A company like RIOT appears in both crypto and data center analysis. Duplicating its full profile in both vaults creates staleness and inconsistency.
  • Full node = company's primary industry. Complete analytical profile lives in ONE place
  • Light node = cross-industry relevance only. Captures the causal chain connecting it to the host vault
  • No financial data in light nodes — computed fresh from XBRL at runtime to avoid stale duplication
  • Light nodes update on the host vault's refresh cycle, not independently

Both Sides of Debates Must Have Equal Evidence

Problem: It's easy to build a vault that looks balanced but actually has a strong bull case with data and a weak bear case with hand-waving. The AI will detect the asymmetry and lean accordingly.
  • Both bull and bear sides must cite specific, sourced evidence at comparable depth
  • If one side's evidence is weaker, that's a research gap to fill — not a signal to lean the other way
  • Market lean is stated explicitly so the AI knows what's consensus and what's contrarian

Sub-Segment-Specific KPIs

Problem: Measuring a neocloud (NBIS) by colocation KPIs (interconnection revenue %) is meaningless. One-size-fits-all metrics miss what actually differentiates performance.
  • Each sub-segment gets its own KPI framework with relevant benchmarks
  • AI matches the company's sub-segment to the right KPI set automatically
  • Benchmark ranges included so AI can assess "good vs bad" — not just report the number
  • Prevents the AI from applying generic financial ratios when sector-specific metrics exist

[DEFLECTION] Flagging in Earnings Intelligence

Problem: Management spin on earnings calls is treated as informational content. Hedging and evasion are filtered out instead of treated as analytical signals.
  • Chicago Booth research: euphemisms and Q&A deflection predict negative outcomes even after controlling for reported financials
  • When management absorbs a question with qualitative signals instead of data, it's flagged [DEFLECTION]
  • Creates a trackable pattern quarter to quarter — new deflections on previously direct topics are a red flag
  • NBIS Q4 2025 example: 5 deflections on capex financing gap, revenue/ARR reconciliation, buffer margins, acquisition price, M&A budget

Two Consumers, One Vault

Problem: The PM needs current-state industry intelligence for sector allocation, but injecting current-state data into the pipeline biases the AI's independent analysis.
  • Structural layer (7 files + company nodes) feeds the pipeline — HOW to think about an industry
  • Living layer (_brief.md + Recent Developments) serves the PM — WHAT is happening now
  • The brief is explicitly excluded from pipeline injection to prevent anchoring on current trends
  • Recent Developments in nodes DO feed the pipeline — they change the analytical framework for that company
  • Different cadences: structural = quarterly, brief = weekly, events = ad-hoc, earnings = after each report

Date-Range Scoping on All Queries

Problem: Re-running vault updates re-discovers information the vault already has, wasting tokens, money, and time.
  • Every node and file carries a last_updated date in YAML frontmatter
  • All Perplexity queries scope to "since {last_updated}" — only fetching new information
  • Earnings queries include prior-quarter context for sequential diff ("focus on NEW or CHANGED")
  • Reduces per-query cost by 30-50% and eliminates redundant re-confirmation of known facts

Key Takeaways

  • 7 structural files + living layer + company nodes — each file answers one question a sector specialist would ask. The living layer (_brief.md) serves the PM without contaminating the pipeline.
  • Full nodes provide complete coverage for companies whose primary industry matches the vault, including earnings intelligence and material event tracking. Light nodes capture only the cross-industry thesis for companies visiting from another sector.
  • Four update modes keep the vault current: structural refresh (quarterly), industry brief (weekly), earnings intelligence (per-report), and material events (ad-hoc). All use date-range scoping to minimize cost.
  • [DEFLECTION] flagging turns management hedging into a trackable analytical signal. Sequential change tracking (STARTED/STOPPED/SHIFTED) reveals narrative shifts quarter to quarter.
  • Every design decision addresses a real failure. Signal direction hiding prevents anchoring. [UNVERIFIED] tags prevent false authority. Sub-segment KPIs prevent apples-to-oranges measurement. Equal-evidence debates prevent embedded bias. Pipeline exclusion of the brief prevents current-state contamination.
  • No enterprise terminals required. The entire data stack runs on FMP (financials), SEC EDGAR (regulatory filings), and Perplexity API (real-time research). Vault updates amortize research cost across runs.
  • The vault is extensible. New industries get the same template. New companies get a full or light node. New debates, signals, or KPIs get added to existing files. Nothing needs to be redesigned.
