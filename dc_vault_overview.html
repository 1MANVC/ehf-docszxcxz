CapEx Accelerating + Grid Power Decelerating
= Site scarcity premium accrues to Layer 3 operators with pre-secured power. Greenfield timelines lengthen, advantaging established operators over new entrants.
Rack Density Accelerating + Liquid Cooling Accelerating
= Structural revenue expansion for Layer 2 cooling and optical interconnect suppliers (COHR, LITE). Permanently higher O&M cost base creates recurring demand.
Three-Signal Squeeze: CapEx Up + Occupancy Up + Grid Down
= Demand outpaces deliverable supply in primary markets. Requirements pushed to secondary hubs. Rewards operators with power-secured alternative sites (IREN, NBIS).
Key Industry Debates — Both Sides, Equal Evidence
1. Will power constraints force structural deceleration before 2028?
Constraints Are Binding
Occupancy 85% → 95%+, grid queues multi-year, 100kW racks increase complexity and capital intensity
Growth Continues
100 GW planned 2026–2030, $5.2T committed, behind-the-meter generation + nuclear bypassing grid
Market lean: Growth continues  •  Resolution: 2027–28 grid approvals + capex guidance
2. Will hyperscalers self-build or will colocation operators capture AI demand?
Self-Build Dominates
70% US DC capacity controlled by hyperscalers, modular 60–80% prefab, end-to-end integration needed
Colocation Accelerates
Record colo deals in 2025–26, APAC colo 19% CAGR, hyperscalers lease when build timelines too long
Market lean: Self-build for primary AI  •  Resolution: H1 2026–27 lease-vs-build disclosures
3. Will cooling & optics suppliers sustain premium margins or face commoditization?
Commoditization Risk
Hyperscaler standardization, modular designs compress differentiation, PUE targets pressure costs
Premiums Sustained
Liquid cooling mandatory at 100kW+, 800G/1.6T optics in shortage through 2027, limited qualified suppliers
Market lean: Premiums sustained near-term  •  Resolution: 2026–27 multi-sourcing cycles

Design Decisions — Why the Vault Works This Way
No Current-State Data in the Vault
Naive approach: "Put everything in one place — industry structure, current trends, latest numbers"
Current data biases the AI. In A/B testing, perspectives parroted vault numbers instead of building their own demand model. Current data also goes stale within 90 days — the AI can't tell the difference between a durable structural insight and a dated figure. The pipeline's own data gathering provides fresh current data every run. The vault teaches HOW to think; the pipeline provides WHAT is happening.
Signal Directions Hidden from Perspectives
Naive approach: "Show perspectives everything so they have full context"
If the bull perspective sees "ACCELERATING" on 4 of 5 signals, it anchors on the direction instead of reasoning independently. Perspectives get the signal definitions and interaction map (the analytical framework) but NOT current direction. Synthesis sees everything — it's post-perspective, so anchoring risk is gone. Frameworks feed analysis. Conclusions feed synthesis.
Light Nodes Have No Financial Metrics
Naive approach: "Include financials for every company in the vault"
Light nodes represent companies whose primary business is elsewhere (LITE's home is optical/photonics, not data centers). Including DC-vault financials risks stale or conflicting numbers with the home vault. Financial data is computed from XBRL at runtime — always fresh, always consistent. Light nodes carry only relationship context: why does this company matter to THIS industry?
Both Sides of Every Debate at Equal Quality
Naive approach: "Present the consensus view with some counterpoints"
If Side A has three sourced data points and Side B has hand-waving, the vault has pre-biased the analysis. Both sides must have comparable evidence quality. The perspective agents position their thesis relative to the debate — if one side is weak, the AI skips it. We include market lean separately so the analyst knows where consensus sits without the vault's evidence quality doing the arguing.
Sub-Segment KPIs with Cross-Application Warnings
Naive approach: "Define standard KPIs for the data center industry"
"Data center" is not one business. GPU utilization rate is meaningless for EQIX. Occupancy rate is meaningless for CoreWeave. The vault defines separate KPI sets per sub-segment and explicitly warns which metrics produce false comparisons when cross-applied. Without this, the AI defaults to generic "data center metrics" — usually colocation KPIs applied to everything.
[DEFLECTION] Flagging in Earnings Intelligence
Naive approach: "Summarize what management said on the earnings call"
Management hedging and evasion is a first-class analytical signal, not noise to filter out. Chicago Booth research: euphemisms and Q&A deflection predict negative outcomes even after controlling for reported financials. The vault flags [DEFLECTION] when management absorbs a question with qualitative signals instead of data — turning spin into a trackable pattern the analyst can monitor quarter to quarter.
